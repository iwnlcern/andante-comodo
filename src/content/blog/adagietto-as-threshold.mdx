The fourth movement of the Fifth Symphony is small only by comparison. Around it stand a funeral march, two vast central movements, and a finale that tries to pull the whole machine back into daylight.

Inside that frame, the Adagietto is a threshold: a room of strings and harp between pressure and release. It shows how a personal site can carry a close reading without requiring much scaffolding.
